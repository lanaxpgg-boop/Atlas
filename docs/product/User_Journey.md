# User Journey

> This document describes how people experience Atlas from their perspective.

Atlas serves two primary user groups:

- Clients
- Independent Beauty Professionals

Every feature should improve at least one step of these journeys.

---

# Client Journey

## 1. Discover

The client opens Atlas looking for inspiration or a beauty professional.

Possible entry points include:

- Feed
- Search
- Categories
- Saved ideas
- Shared links
- Professional profiles

Goal:

Find something interesting.

---

## 2. Explore

The client explores content.

They may:

- Watch photos or videos
- Open portfolios
- Save ideas
- Like content
- Share content
- Read comments

Goal:

Build confidence.

---

## 3. Find the Right Professional

The client searches for professionals using filters.

Examples:

- Location
- Distance
- Price
- Style
- Rating
- Availability
- Service type

Goal:

Create a shortlist.

---

## 4. Evaluate

The client compares professionals.

Signals include:

- Portfolio quality
- Reviews
- Experience
- Profile completeness
- Response time
- Verification

Goal:

Choose with confidence.

---

## 5. Book

The client books an appointment.

Possible steps:

- Choose service
- Choose date
- Choose time
- Add notes
- Confirm booking

Goal:

Book in less than two minutes.

---

## 6. Appointment

The appointment takes place.

Atlas supports reminders, communication, and updates.

Goal:

A smooth experience.

---

## 7. Review

After the appointment the client can:

- Leave a review
- Upload photos
- Rate the experience
- Rebook

Goal:

Strengthen trust for future clients.

---

## 8. Relationship

Atlas encourages long-term relationships.

Clients should naturally return to professionals they trust.

Success is not one booking.

Success is repeat bookings.

---

# Professional Journey

## 1. Join Atlas

Professional creates an account.

Goal:

Become visible.

---

## 2. Build Profile

The professional adds:

- Portfolio
- Services
- Prices
- Biography
- Location
- Working hours
- Contact information

Goal:

Create trust.

---

## 3. Publish Work

The professional shares completed work.

Content appears in:

- Feed
- Search
- Recommendations
- Profile

Goal:

Increase visibility.

---

## 4. Receive Discovery

Potential clients discover the professional.

They may:

- View profile
- Save work
- Follow
- Contact
- Book

Goal:

Generate demand.

---

## 5. Manage Bookings

The professional manages:

- Calendar
- Availability
- Requests
- Cancellations
- Client communication

Goal:

Operate efficiently.

---

## 6. Deliver Great Service

Appointments create reputation.

Great work leads to:

- Reviews
- Repeat bookings
- Referrals

Goal:

Grow organically.

---

## 7. Grow Business

Atlas provides tools that help professionals:

- Understand performance
- Improve visibility
- Increase bookings
- Build loyal clients

Goal:

Build a sustainable business.

---

# Shared Journey

Clients succeed when they discover professionals they trust.

Professionals succeed when discovery becomes bookings.

Atlas succeeds only when both happen together.

---

# Journey Principles

Every feature should reduce friction.

Every interaction should increase trust.

Every booking should strengthen long-term relationships.

Growth should be a consequence of delivering value.

---

# North Star

Atlas is successful when finding and booking the right beauty professional becomes as natural as searching for a place on Google Maps or discovering content on Instagram.
