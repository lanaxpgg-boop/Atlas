# Feed PRD

Version: 1.0

---

# 1. Purpose

The Atlas Feed helps clients discover beauty inspiration and connect that inspiration with real professionals who can deliver it.

The feed is designed to inspire, build trust, and lead naturally toward booking.

---

# 2. Problem Statement

Today, beauty inspiration and booking are disconnected.

Users discover ideas on social media but must manually search for professionals who can recreate them.

Professionals publish work on social platforms but struggle to convert attention into bookings.

Atlas connects inspiration directly to professionals.

---

# 3. Goals

The Feed should:

- Inspire users.
- Surface relevant local professionals.
- Help users save ideas.
- Increase profile visits.
- Drive bookings.
- Encourage repeat visits.

---

# 4. Non-Goals

The Feed is not designed to maximize screen time.

It is not a general entertainment platform.

It is not intended to replace every social media experience.

Its primary objective is to connect inspiration with trusted professionals.

---

# 5. User Stories

### As a client I want to:

- Scroll through beautiful work.
- Discover styles I like.
- Save ideas.
- Follow professionals.
- Share inspiration.
- Open the professional profile.
- Book an appointment.

### As a professional I want to:

- Publish my work.
- Reach nearby clients.
- Build an audience.
- Convert views into bookings.
- Grow my reputation.

---

# 6. Functional Requirements

The Feed supports:

- Photo posts
- Video posts (future)
- Likes
- Comments
- Saves
- Shares
- Following professionals
- Profile previews
- Direct navigation to booking
- Infinite scrolling
- Personalized ranking
- Location-aware recommendations

---

# 7. User Flow

Client opens Atlas

↓

Feed loads

↓

Client scrolls

↓

Interesting work appears

↓

Client opens the professional profile

↓

Portfolio review

↓

Booking starts

---

# 8. Success Metrics

Primary metrics:

- Feed → Profile conversion rate
- Profile → Booking conversion rate
- Save rate
- Follow rate

Supporting metrics:

- Session frequency
- Return rate
- Content engagement
- Booking attribution

---

# 9. Edge Cases

- New user with no history
- New professional with few posts
- Spam content
- Duplicate uploads
- Inappropriate content
- Low-quality images
- Fake engagement
