# Discovery PRD

> Version: 1.0

---

# 1. Purpose

Discovery is the entry point of Atlas.

Its purpose is to help clients discover beauty professionals and beauty inspiration quickly, confidently, and naturally.

For professionals, Discovery exists to increase visibility and generate qualified bookings.

---

# 2. Problem Statement

Today, discovering beauty professionals is fragmented.

Clients typically:

- Scroll Instagram
- Search hashtags
- Ask friends
- Check Google Maps
- Read reviews elsewhere
- Send DMs to ask about availability

Professionals struggle because their work is spread across multiple platforms.

Atlas should unify discovery into one experience.

---

# 3. Goals

Discovery should help users:

- Find professionals nearby
- Discover new styles
- Explore portfolios
- Save inspiration
- Build trust before booking
- Reach booking with minimal friction

---

# 4. Non-Goals

Discovery is not intended to:

- Replace messaging
- Replace booking
- Replace portfolio management

Its responsibility is discovery only.

---

# 5. User Stories

As a client I want to:

- discover new nail ideas
- discover professionals nearby
- discover professionals matching my preferred style
- save inspiration
- compare professionals
- move naturally into booking

As a professional I want to:

- be discovered
- reach local clients
- showcase my work
- receive more profile visits
- receive more bookings

---

# 6. Functional Requirements

Discovery should provide:

- Personalized feed
- Local recommendations
- Trending content
- Nearby professionals
- Category browsing
- Seasonal inspiration
- Style-based discovery
- Saved collections
- Professional profile previews
- Search integration

---

# 7. User Flow

Client opens Atlas

↓

Feed opens

↓

User scrolls

↓

Interesting content appears

↓

Profile opened

↓

Portfolio explored

↓

Professional selected

↓

Booking begins

---

# 8. Success Metrics

- Feed engagement
- Saves
- Profile opens
- Time to first profile view
- Search-to-profile conversion
- Profile-to-booking conversion

---

# 9. Edge Cases

- New users without history
- New professionals without followers
- Sparse local supply
- Low-quality portfolios
- Duplicate content
- Fake engagement

---

Possible future improvements:

- Event-based recommendations
