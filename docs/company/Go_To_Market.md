# Go-To-Market Strategy

> Atlas will grow city by city by becoming the most trusted platform for discovering and booking independent beauty professionals.

---

# Core Strategy

Atlas will not launch everywhere.

Marketplace businesses succeed through density, not geographic coverage.

Our goal is to dominate one local market before expanding to the next.

---

# Initial Market

Atlas begins with one category:

- Independent Nail Professionals

Why nails?

- High booking frequency
- Highly visual work
- Strong social sharing
- Large independent workforce
- Existing discovery and booking friction
- Strong repeat-customer behavior

---

# Geographic Expansion

Expansion follows this sequence:

City

↓

Region

↓

Country

↓

International

A city is considered successful before expansion only when supply and demand are healthy.

---

# Supply First

Marketplace quality depends on professionals.

Atlas will prioritize attracting high-quality professionals before investing heavily in client acquisition.

Without supply, there is no marketplace.

---

# Professional Acquisition

Our first professionals are our most important users.

Early acquisition channels include:

- Instagram outreach
- TikTok outreach
- Local Facebook groups
- Beauty schools
- Professional referrals
- Community ambassadors
- Local events

Early professionals receive white-glove onboarding and direct support.

---

# Client Acquisition

Once enough professionals are active, Atlas begins acquiring clients.

Channels include:

- Organic social media
- Local influencers
- Referral programs
- SEO
- Local search
- Paid advertising (when unit economics are proven)
- Word of mouth

---

# Solving the Cold Start Problem

Atlas solves marketplace cold start by focusing on density instead of scale.

Principles:

- One city at a time
- One category at a time
- One trusted community at a time

A smaller marketplace with high activity is more valuable than a large marketplace with little engagement.

---

# Marketplace Liquidity

The goal is to maximize successful matches between clients and professionals.

Key indicators include:

- Searches that lead to profile views
- Profile views that lead to bookings
- Booking completion rate
- Repeat bookings
- Time to first booking for new professionals

---

# Community Strategy

Atlas is not only software.

Atlas is a professional community.

Professionals should feel they are building their business through Atlas, not simply using another application.

Trust, education, and long-term relationships are strategic assets.

---

# Expansion Criteria

Atlas expands into a new city only after meeting predefined success metrics.

Examples include:

- Active professionals
- Monthly bookings
- Client retention
- Professional retention
- Marketplace liquidity
- Customer satisfaction

---

# Future Expansion

Once the nail category reaches product-market fit, Atlas may expand into adjacent beauty categories:

- Lash Artists
- Brow Artists
- Hair Stylists
- Barbers
- Estheticians
- Makeup Artists

Each expansion must validate that workflows and economics remain aligned with the Atlas platform.

---

# Success Metrics

Go-to-market success is measured by:

- Professional retention
- Client retention
- Booking growth
- Repeat bookings
- Marketplace liquidity
- Referral rate
- Customer satisfaction

Growth without retention is not considered success.

---

# Guiding Principle

Atlas grows by earning trust within local beauty communities.

Strong local marketplaces create the foundation for long-term global expansion.
